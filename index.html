I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Center for Neural Science at New York University and a Junior Fellow of the Simons Society of Fellows. My research employs psychophysical experiments, computational modeling, and functional neuroimaging to study the neural basis of cognition.

I am also interested in developing and improving tools for computational data analysis. I am the creator of seaborn, a Python data visualization library, and I have originated or contributed to several other open source projects.

I received my PhD from Stanford University, where I worked with Anthony Wagner. Before graduate school, I was a research assistant in John Gabrieli's lab at MIT. My undergraduate degree is from Amherst College, where I studied philosophy and neuroscience.
